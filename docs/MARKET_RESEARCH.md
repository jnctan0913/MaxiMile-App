## STEP 1 — Market Definition

### Primary Market Category

**Personal Finance Optimization Tools — Credit Card Rewards Maximization (Niche Vertical)**

This is a **hybrid/emerging category** that sits at the intersection of:
- Personal finance management (expense tracking)
- Credit card comparison platforms (product selection)
- Loyalty program optimization (rewards maximization)

No established category label exists because no product currently operates in this exact space in Singapore. The closest global precedent is the emerging "card-linked rewards optimization" category in the US (CardPointers, MaxRewards, Kudos), which has attracted US$13M+ in collective funding as of 2024.

### Customer Segments

| Segment | Size (Est.) | Profile |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **Primary: Active Miles Optimizers** | ~100,000–200,000 | Hold 3–7 miles cards; read MileLion/Suitesmile; track spending manually; financially literate PMETs aged 25–45 |
| **Secondary: Passive Miles Holders** | ~100,000–200,000 | Hold 1–2 miles cards; know they should optimize but find it too complex; default to one "safe" card |
| **Tertiary: Miles-Curious Professionals** | ~100,000+ | High-income PMETs who travel frequently but haven't yet engaged with miles optimization |

### Core Problem Being Solved

Urban working professionals in Singapore who use multiple credit cards for airline miles **cannot effectively track complex, constantly-changing card rules** (category-specific earn rates, monthly bonus caps, MCC exclusions, promotional periods) **across 3–7 cards in real-time at the point of payment**. This results in:

- **Thousands of lost miles annually** (worth SGD 200–500+ in flights)
- **Decision fatigue and anxiety** at checkout
- **User abandonment** — many give up miles optimization entirely due to complexity

### Value Proposition

**Turn complex credit card rules into simple, real-time, one-tap decisions** — automatically recommending the optimal credit card at the point of payment based on spend category, remaining bonus cap, and user preferences.

### Why This Category Is Emerging/Hybrid

1. **No direct precedent in Singapore**: Comparison sites (SingSaver, MoneySmart) help users *choose* cards but not *use* them optimally. Expense trackers (Seedly) track spending but don't recommend cards. Miles blogs (MileLion) inform but require manual application.
2. **US validation but no APAC entry**: CardPointers (Apple App of the Day), MaxRewards (US$3M seed), and Kudos (US$10.2M Series A from QED Investors) have proven the model in the US but none support Singapore cards.
3. **Category creation opportunity**: MaxiMile would be the first product in Singapore to combine portfolio-aware card recommendations + real-time cap tracking + point-of-payment optimization.

### Competitive Analysis Summary

#### Market Saturation Level: **LOW**

No direct competitor exists in Singapore for real-time, portfolio-aware card optimization at the point of payment. The market has adjacent players (comparison sites, expense trackers, blogs) but none operating in this specific niche. The US has validated the model (CardPointers, MaxRewards, Kudos raised US$13M+ collectively), but nobody has built it for Singapore's credit card ecosystem.

#### Competitive Gaps

1. **No real-time cap tracking**: No existing product tracks cumulative spending against monthly bonus caps across multiple cards
2. **No portfolio-aware recommendations**: Comparison sites recommend cards generically, not based on the user's actual card portfolio
3. **No point-of-payment optimization**: All existing solutions require the user to proactively seek information; none deliver at the moment of decision
4. **No MCC-aware merchant matching**: No Singapore app maps specific merchants to spending categories for automatic card recommendation
5. **No cross-bank optimization**: Banks' own apps only optimize within their own card portfolio

#### Differentiation Opportunities

1. **First-mover advantage in Singapore** — the specific niche is unoccupied
2. **Community-driven launch** — leverage MileLion's 31K+ Telegram members and broader ecosystem
3. **Complementary positioning** — partner with (not compete against) blogs, comparison sites, and Kris+
4. **Trust-first approach** — no affiliate revenue in v1; pure user-value positioning
5. **Data moat** — accumulated spending pattern data becomes increasingly valuable over time

### 2. MAS Licensing Requirements

**Critical question**: Does MaxiMile require a Financial Adviser's (FA) licence from MAS?

**Analysis based on MAS Guidelines on Provision of Financial Advisory Service (July 2019):**

MAS uses a two-stage test:
1. Does the activity amount to providing financial advice?
2. Is the person carrying on a business in such activity?

**Key finding**: The FAA defines "investment product" as capital markets products, life policies, and structured deposits. **Credit cards are NOT investment products under the FAA.** Therefore, recommending credit cards based on spending patterns is **less likely** to fall within the strict scope of the FAA.

| Scenario | MAS Assessment | MaxiMile Applicability |
|----------|----------------|----------------------|
| Aggregator showing objective product comparisons | **Not likely financial advice** | MaxiMile's card rules database falls here |
| Tool shortlisting products based on user's personal financial details | **More likely financial advice** | MaxiMile's personalized recommendations could approach this |
| Credit card recommendation specifically | **Lower risk** — credit cards are not "investment products" under FAA | Favorable for MaxiMile |

**If FA licensing were required**: S$250,000–500,000 base capital; minimum 2 directors, 3 full-time SG-based staff with 5+ years experience; professional indemnity insurance.

**Recommendation**: Position as an **informational tool** providing factual data (earn rates, caps, MCC mappings) rather than "financial advice." Seek legal opinion pre-launch.

**Sources**: [MAS Guidelines on FA Service (Jul 2019)](https://www.mas.gov.sg/-/media/MAS/Regulations-and-Financial-Stability/Regulations-Guidance-and-Licensing/Financial-Advisers/Guidelines/Guidelines-on-Provision-of-Financial-Advisory-Service-10-July-2019.pdf); [Allen & Gledhill — MAS Issues Guidelines](https://www.allenandgledhill.com/sg/publication/articles/12350/mas-issues-guidelines-on-provision-of-financial-advisory-service) (2019)

### 5. Verdict: **GO** (with conditions)

**Recommendation: GO — proceed with MVP development.**

**Justification:**

The business opportunity is commercially viable based on:

1. **Proven demand**: Multiple independent data sources confirm strong, measurable demand — 70M+ lifetime pageviews on miles content, 31K+ community members, 85% survey interest, and direct forum requests for exactly this product.

2. **Clear competitive white space**: No direct competitor in Singapore. The market gap is validated by US comparables (CardPointers, Kudos) that have raised significant funding, proving the model works.

3. **Favorable timing**: The KrisFlyer devaluation (Nov 2025) has intensified the need for optimization. Bank card products are becoming more complex, increasing the value of an automated solution.

4. **Community-driven GTM**: The MileLion ecosystem (948K monthly visits, 31K+ Telegram) provides a low-CAC launch channel that most startups cannot access.

5. **Manageable regulatory risk**: Credit cards are not "investment products" under MAS FAA; PDPA compliance is standard; legal review is recommended but unlikely to be blocking.

**Conditions for GO:**

- **Monetization model should be hybrid** (freemium + future affiliate), not pure subscription — given Singapore's strong expectation of free personal finance tools and significant subscription fatigue (57% feel over-subscribed)
- **Manual transaction logging UX must be validated** before full build — this is the #1 execution risk for retention
- **MileLion partnership should be secured** before or at launch — this is the #1 distribution lever
- **Legal opinion on MAS positioning** should be obtained before launch
- **Regional expansion roadmap** (Malaysia, Hong Kong) should be planned from day one to address the Singapore TAM ceiling

**If manual logging UX testing fails** (users won't log consistently), the recommendation shifts to **PIVOT** — consider building a browser extension or bank SMS parser instead of a manual-input mobile app.
